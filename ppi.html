What is PPI?

Understanding Payment Protection Insurance

Payment Protection Insurance

PPI was designed to cover loan repayments if you couldn't work due to illness, unemployment, or accident.

However, millions of policies were mis-sold to people who didn't want them, couldn't use them, or didn't even know they had them.

Was Your PPI Mis-Sold?

PPI was commonly mis-sold if:

  • You were told it was compulsory
  • You weren't told about exclusions
  • You were self-employed or retired
  • You had pre-existing medical conditions
  • It was added without your knowledge

Check Your Eligibility

Find out if you can make a PPI claim

You May Be Eligible If:

  • You had a loan, credit card, or mortgage between 1990-2010
  • PPI was added to your agreement
  • You weren't properly informed about the policy
  • You were pressured into taking PPI
  • The policy didn't cover your circumstances

Types of Credit with PPI:

  • Credit Cards
  • Mortgages
  • Car Loans
  • Personal Loans
  • Store Cards
